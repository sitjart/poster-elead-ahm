THE PATTERN

Women hold the coordination & "glue" roles. Men hold the seats with budgets, strategy and sponsorship.

ELEAD targets the exact transition point — a structured mentoring, peer-learning and curated training pathway, not a generic leadership course.

1Personal mentoring1:1 with senior leaders, academia & industry
2Peer mentoringSmall confidential groups — most-cited element
3Professional trainingExternal experts; some sessions open to all ELIXIR

Where ELEAD goes next

Three live tracks. None individually sufficient. Together, the path to ELEAD 3.0 and beyond.

TRACK 1

Inside ELIXIR

  • Proposing an ELEAD-related activity in the People CoS 2027–28. Selection is community-driven and competitive.
  • Likely focus 2027–28: conceptual development & alumni engagement rather than a new cohort.
  • Discussions with the ELIXIR Hub on positioning for the 2029+ five-year programme.
  • Considering a matching-funds model with Hub + Node backing for ELEAD 3.0.

Risk: as one activity inside a larger CoS, ELEAD loses visibility and strategic focus.

TRACK 2

Across infrastructures

  • EOSC — exploring funding alignment with leadership & capacity-building strands.
  • Pistoia Alliance — initial discussions on shared mentoring & diversity frameworks.
  • Instruct-ERIC — early conversations on cross-RI leadership exchange.
  • Euro-BioImaging & other ESFRI infrastructures — invited into the conversation.

Status: strong interest, no firm commitments yet. Conversations live; partners welcome.

TRACK 3

In your Node

  • ELIXIR-UK — national grant submitted for an ELEAD-based peer-mentoring programme for under-represented dRTPs.
  • EMBL-EBI — Mallory Freeberg has launched a peer-mentoring group for new Team Leads, modelled on ELEAD.
  • AHM 2025 workshop — ELIXIR-GR, ELIXIR-CH and ELIXIR-ES already exploring national-level spin-offs.
  • Materials are open; the coordination team will support Nodes adapting the model locally.

Take it home. If ELEAD won't return as a Hub programme, it can still return as your Node's programme.

WHAT WE'RE ASKING FOR AT THIS AHM

Three conversations.

  1. If you fund or shape CoS priorities — talk to us about ELEAD's place in 2027–28 and 2029+.
  2. If you sit across infrastructures — introduce us to your EOSC / Pistoia / RI counterparts.
  3. If you lead a Node — let's plan an ELEAD instance for your community. Materials are ready.
