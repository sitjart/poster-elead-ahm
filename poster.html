Why ELEAD exists

Leadership in distributed research infrastructures takes place without formal authority and across institutional and national boundaries. ELEAD targets the exact transition where the pipeline thins.

WHY THIS MATTERS NOW

Diverse teams outperform — but only when paired with psychological safety.

  • The business case is settled. McKinsey's 2023 cross-sector evidence is mirrored in STEM (British Science Association, 2025): inclusive leadership drives innovation, retention and resilience.
  • The academic case is documented. OECD (2024) and peer-reviewed analyses of academia show persistent gaps in career pathways, mobility and senior representation — despite parity at entry.
  • Diversity without safety underperforms. Edmondson (HBR, 2022) and Google's Project Aristotle: the highest-performing teams pair difference with the freedom to speak up.

How ELEAD works: three pillars — 1:1 mentoring with senior leaders, peer mentoring in small confidential groups, and professional training delivered by external experts.

Where ELEAD goes next

With internal funding closed, ELEAD's future depends on adoption. Two routes. One question to answer this week.

ACROSS INFRASTRUCTURES

Do we want to lead — or follow?

ELIXIR has built one of the most rigorous EDI leadership programmes in European research infrastructure. Several peer RIs have done less and published more — policies about policies, statements about statements.

ELEAD has measurable outcomes, four impact case studies, a logic model, and an external Scientific Advisory Board. Active conversations are open:

  • EOSC — leadership & capacity-building strands
  • Pistoia Alliance — shared mentoring & diversity frameworks
  • Instruct-ERIC — cross-RI leadership exchange
  • Euro-BioImaging and other ESFRI infrastructures
INSIDE NODES

Take it home.

ELEAD doesn't need to be run centrally to continue. It can return as your Node's programme. Materials are open; the coordination team supports adaptation.

  • ELIXIR-UK — national grant submitted for peer-mentoring for under-represented dRTPs
  • EMBL-EBI — Mallory Freeberg's Team-Lead peer-mentoring group, live
  • ELIXIR-GR / CH / ES — national spin-offs explored at AHM 2025
TALK TO US TODAY

Three conversations.

  1. If you work across infrastructures — connect us with your EOSC, Pistoia or RI counterparts.
  2. If you lead a Node — let's plan an ELEAD instance for your community. Materials are ready.
  3. If you've been a mentor or mentee — share your story; help shape what ELEAD becomes next.
